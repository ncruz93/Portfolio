Hiking & Travel

I decided to do hiking and travel under one category as they overlap a fair amount. Here is a table to show where I've been somewhere, along with when and how long!

PLACE: MONTH: TIME SPENT:
Hawaii February 1 week
Florida June 1 week
Puerto Rico August 2 weeks
Maine September 3 days
DC November 5 days

I will put my favorite places in order below:

  1. Hawaii
  2. Puerto Rico
  3. Maine
  4. DC
  5. Florida

So I have been around a fair amount this last year. I still have one more stop to make on a cruise before it's over, but other than that I may as well give you a brief discription of the places I've been. I'll go in order of least favorite to favorite.

5. Florida

Okay, so I know that saying Florida was my least favorite place to go to is almost sacrilegious, but hear me out. I have been there half a dozen times due to family, and this trip doubled as part vacation, part agonizing 30 hour travel in a moving truck to help my brother move there from Boston. The weather was great though, and Universal was fun (as always)

4. DC

Going to DC was great. It was the first time I had ever been there, and there were so many free museums to go to. The weather was great (even though it was November), and there was almost too much to do. Unfortunately, while I was in the area for 5 days, I really only had 2 days to actually explore, leaving me wanting more.

3. Maine

Maine was fantastic for me. We were able to bring the dogs as the state is incredibly dog friendly, go on hikes, and even have the best milkshakes I have ever had at a place in Portland called Duckfat. It really was just refreshing to get out of the city and enjoy nature.

2. Puerto Rico

Stunning place, had the most beautiful beach I had ever been to, stunning mountains, and delicious Pina Coladas (which apparently originate from there). The only thing I wish there was more of was diversity of food. Everything was pretty much the same everywhere you went, which can get tiring after a while.

1. Hawaii

They call it paridise for a reason. Perfect weather, rainforest, mountains, beaches, culture, history, snorkeling, ziplining, luaus. I can't name something that I didn't love while there. On the plane back I was already wondering when I would visit again.
